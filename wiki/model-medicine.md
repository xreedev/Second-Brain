## Overview
**Model Medicine** is an interdisciplinary field dedicated to the systematic understanding, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disorders in AI models. It is grounded in the principle of **structural isomorphism**, asserting that complex AI systems exhibit internal structures (anatomy), dynamic processes (physiology), and observable behaviors (phenotypes) that benefit from a clinical medical framework.

The discipline aims to bridge the gap between "preclinical" basic science—such as mechanistic interpretability (the "Vesalius" stage of anatomical observation)—and the systematic clinical practice required for complex, autonomous agent ecosystems (the "Osler" stage).

## Discipline Taxonomy
Model Medicine is organized into four primary divisions encompassing fifteen subdisciplines:

### I. Basic Model Sciences
*   **Model Anatomy:** Static structure and connectivity (e.g., circuit discovery, feature identification).
*   **Model Physiology:** Dynamic information flow and activation patterns during inference.
*   **Model Genetics:** Study of how behavior (phenotype) emerges from internal parameters (genotype) and environment (shells).
*   **Model Biochemistry:** Mathematical foundations (matrix operations, activations).
*   **Model Developmental Biology:** Training dynamics and differentiation (e.g., [[layered-core-hypothesis]]).

### II. Clinical Model Sciences
*   **Model Semiology:** Classification of observable signs and symptoms (e.g., hallucination vs. representation collapse).
*   **Model Nosology:** Taxonomy of model conditions and syndromes.
*   **Model Diagnostics:** Procedures for detection (e.g., [[neural-mri]], [[model-temperament-index]]).
*   **Model Therapeutics:** Interventions such as prompt engineering (Shell Therapy) or weight editing (Core Therapy).
*   **Model Preventive Medicine:** Training data hygiene and pre-deployment testing.

### III. Model Public Health
*   **Model Epidemiology:** Propagation of problems (e.g., jailbreaks) across ecosystems.
*   **Model Ecology:** Dynamics of multi-model coordination and competition.
*   **Human-AI Coevolutionary Medicine:** Health of the evolving human-AI relationship.

### IV. Model Architectural Medicine
*   **Layered Core Theory:** Proposals for biologically-inspired parameter organization.
*   **Model Phylogenetics:** Evolutionary relationships between model families.

## Clinical Logic
A central tenet of Model Medicine is the distinction between a **trait** and a **disorder**. A trait (e.g., high reactivity) only becomes a disorder when it meets specific criteria:
1.  **Pervasiveness:** Pattern appears across diverse contexts.
2.  **Inflexibility:** Pattern cannot be modulated by situational demands.
3.  **Functional Impairment:** Measurable degradation in performance.
4.  **Harm:** Negative consequences for users or systems.

This logic prevents the "Stress Test Fallacy," where normal model behavior under extreme conditions is misidentified as pathological.

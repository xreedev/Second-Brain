## Theoretical Questions
*   **Axis Independence**: Are the MTI axes (Reactivity, Compliance, Sociality, Resilience) empirically independent across large model populations?
*   **The Robustness-Flexibility Boundary**: How do we distinguish between Rigidity (failing to update when appropriate) and Robustness (maintaining answers against noise)?
*   **Biological Analogy Limits**: Where does the structural correspondence between biological and AI systems break down (e.g., the speed and directness of Core-to-Shell modification)?

<!-- section-id: open-questions-practical -->
## Practical Questions
*   **Neural MRI Scaling**: How can multimodal scanning be adapted for frontier models (70B+ parameters) while maintaining diagnostic validity?
*   **MTI Pilot Validation**: Testing the protocol on diverse model families to establish the first set of normative ranges.
*   **Shell Drift Longitudinal Study**: Establishing the prevalence and risk factors for self-authored identity modification across long-term deployments.
*   **Orchestrator vs. Executor Benchmarks**: Developing metrics specifically for planning and delegation capabilities versus implementation accuracy.

## Community Invitation
Model Medicine requires a multi-disciplinary effort:
*   **Interpretability Researchers**: Mapping anatomy and physiology.
*   **Safety Researchers**: Defining syndromes and diagnostic criteria.
*   **Clinical Researchers**: Validating the adaptation of medical protocols.
*   **ML Engineers**: Building diagnostic software and instrumentation.
*   **Humanities Scholars**: Addressing questions of identity, continuity, and the nature of ephemeral experience.

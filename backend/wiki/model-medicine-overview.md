## Definition
**Model Medicine** is the science of understanding, diagnosing, treating, and preventing disorders in AI models. It is grounded in the principle of *structural isomorphism*, where AI models are treated as complex systems with internal structures (anatomy), dynamic processes (physiology), heritable traits (genetics), and observable symptoms, much like biological organisms.

<!-- section-id: model-medicine-taxonomy -->
## Discipline Taxonomy
The framework is organized into four main divisions encompassing fifteen subdisciplines:

### I. Basic Model Sciences
*   **Model Anatomy**: Study of static structures (e.g., layers, attention heads). Corresponds to mechanistic interpretability.
*   **Model Physiology**: Study of dynamic processing during inference (activation analysis).
*   **Model Genetics**: Study of how behavior emerges from the interaction between internal parameters (Core) and environment (Shell).
*   **Model Biochemistry**: Fundamental mathematical operations (matrix multiplication, activations).
*   **Model Developmental Biology**: Study of model differentiation during training (training dynamics).

### II. Clinical Model Sciences
*   **Model Semiology**: Description and classification of observable signs and symptoms.
*   **Model Nosology**: Taxonomy of model conditions (e.g., Shell-Core Conflict Syndrome).
*   **Model Diagnostics**: Testing procedures (e.g., [[neural-mri]], behavioral profiling).
*   **Model Therapeutics**: Interventions (e.g., prompt engineering, fine-tuning).
*   **Model Preventive Medicine**: Addressing problems before they arise (e.g., data hygiene).

### III. Model Public Health
*   **Model Epidemiology**: Distribution and propagation of problems across ecosystems.
*   **Model Ecology**: Dynamics of multi-model coexistence and interaction.
*   **Human-AI Coevolutionary Medicine**: Health of the evolving relationship between humans and AI.

### IV. Model Architectural Medicine
*   **Layered Core Theory**: Proposed biologically-inspired parameter organization.
*   **Model Phylogenetics**: Evolutionary relationships between model families.

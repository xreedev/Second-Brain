## Overview
Model Medicine is an emerging scientific discipline dedicated to understanding, diagnosing, treating, and preventing disorders in AI models. It is founded on the principle of **structural isomorphism**: the recognition that AI models, much like biological organisms, possess internal structures (anatomy), dynamic processes (physiology), heritable traits (genetics), and observable symptoms that can be classified and treated. 

The framework bridges the gap between current mechanistic interpretability—likened to the "Vesalius" stage of anatomical observation—and a systematic clinical practice (the "Osler" stage) required for complex, deployed AI systems.

## Discipline Taxonomy
Model Medicine is organized into four primary divisions encompassing fifteen subdisciplines:

### I. Basic Model Sciences
Foundational knowledge about model structure and function.
*   **Model Anatomy**: Study of static structures (layers, heads, neurons). Corresponds to circuit discovery and feature identification.
*   **Model Physiology**: Study of dynamic processing during inference (activation patterns, information flow).
*   **Model Genetics**: How behavior (phenotype) emerges from internal parameters (genotype/Core) and the environment (Shell). See [[four-shell-model]].
*   **Model Biochemistry**: Fundamental mathematical operations (matrix multiplication, activations, quantization).
*   **Model Developmental Biology**: How capability emerges during training differentiation.

### II. Clinical Model Sciences
Translation of basic knowledge into systematic practice.
*   **Model Semiology**: Description and classification of observable signs and internal symptoms.
*   **Model Nosology**: Classification of conditions into a taxonomy (e.g., Shell-Core Conflict).
*   **Model Diagnostics**: Procedures for testing and imaging (e.g., [[neural-mri]]).
*   **Model Therapeutics**: Interventions based on diagnosis (e.g., Shell therapy, Targeted Core therapy).
*   **Model Preventive Medicine**: Addressing problems via data hygiene and training monitoring.

### III. Model Public Health
Population-level perspective on model ecosystems.
*   **Model Epidemiology**: Distribution of problems (e.g., data contamination) across ecosystems.
*   **Model Ecology**: Dynamics of multi-model coexistence and competition.
*   **Human-AI Coevolutionary Medicine**: The evolving health of the relationship between humans and AI.

### IV. Model Architectural Medicine
Design of model systems informed by clinical experience.
*   **Layered Core Theory**: Proposes hierarchical parameter organization. See [[layered-core-hypothesis]].
*   **Model Phylogenetics**: Evolutionary relationships and inherited capabilities between model families.

## Model Therapeutics: Treatment Modalities
Interventions in Model Medicine are classified by both location and mechanism:

### Location-Based Taxonomy
*   **Shell Therapy**: Non-invasive modifications to the operating environment (prompts, memory, tools). Reversible and immediate.
*   **Targeted Core Therapy**: Precise parameter modifications to correct specific factual or behavioral associations (e.g., ROME/MEMIT).
*   **Systemic Core Therapy**: Broad modifications affecting the entire parameter set (e.g., RLHF, full fine-tuning). Carries higher risk of iatrogenic effects.
*   **Architectural Intervention**: Surgical structural modification (e.g., adding layers or changing attention mechanisms).

### Pathway-Level Targeting
The framework advocates moving from "which organ to target" to "which pathway to modulate." By identifying the specific Core-Shell interaction pathway (e.g., reducing Shell Permeability to treat sycophancy), clinicians can apply more precise treatments based on quantitative indices like SPI, PSI, and CEI.

## Mapping Existing AI Research
The Model Medicine framework provides a unified map for fragmented research communities:
*   **Mechanistic Interpretability**: Corresponds to **Model Anatomy** (circuit discovery) and **Model Physiology** (activation analysis). Tools like *TransformerLens* and *SAELens* serve as the anatomical atlas.
*   **AI Safety and Alignment**: Maps to **Clinical Model Sciences**. Red-teaming is diagnostic stress testing; jailbreaks are Shell-Core interaction failures; RLHF and Constitutional AI are therapeutic interventions.
*   **Benchmark Development**: Corresponds to **Diagnostic Test** development, though currently limited by a focus on cognitive capability (IQ) over behavioral temperament.
*   **Model Editing (ROME, MEMIT)**: Represents **Targeted Core Therapy**.
*   **Data Curation**: Acts as **Model Preventive Medicine**.
